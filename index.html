Abstract

This article introduces an integrated platform, namely DexPlat, specifically designed to facilitate the transfer of human dexterous manipulations to robot hands through demonstration in different conditions. It employs an effective mapping method that enables the real-time transfer of human hand gestures to various dexterous robot hands using only a monocular camera, even in environments with varying levels of shade and self-obscuring elements. By incorporating pose and angle constraints, the high degree-of-freedom (DOF) dexterous hand, equipped with a root swinging function for the fingers, can achieve human-like postures while avoiding finger collisions. The dexterous hand mapping algorithm has been enhanced to address DOF discrepancies between human and robotic hands, significantly improving real-time skill transfer. This method has demonstrated superior performance in both simulated and physical environments
